Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

What is neuroplasticity?

It was long thought that the brain was hard-wired and unable to change, however it has since been discovered that we can create new pathways and connections between neurons in the brain. Over time these connections become thicker with more neurons and old pathways that aren’t used get pruned and trimmed off.

How does understanding the concepts of neuroplasticity benefit learning?

Interestingly if you understand the principles of neuroplasticity you are more likely to succeed. One study showed that children who understood the brain was plastic and their intelligence wasn’t predetermined were more likely to get better grades and have better morale. Knowing that one is fully capable of learning new things and changing ones brain makes motivation to do things easier to come by as just practising and trying (regardless of if something is a ‘success’ or ‘fail’) is enough to be able to change the brain to learn new things.

How could you engage with these principles for your own benefit?

Repetition, use it or lose it, and use it and improve it are all super relevant in learning programming as the more you to things the more ‘muscle memory’ you have and the more your brain makes and solidifies those learning pathways.

Ways to improve your neuroplasticity

There are a handful of vitamins that help to support neuroplasticity. These are: magnesium, selenium and omega-3. Regular, good sleep and exercise (both low and high intensity exercise will boost the production of BDNF (a particular chemical that assists with the firing or neurons)). Learning a new language, making art, playing an instrument, and travelling are also things that can increase neuroplasticity. Reducing stress and increasing meditation can also boost neuroplasticity

Growth Mindset

What is growth mindset and why is it relevant?

Growth mindset is when an individual believes their talents can be developed through hard work, good strategies, and input from others. They tend to achieve more than these with a more fixed mindset. This relates closely to neuroplasticity. Understanding that our brains are plastic and can be changed for the better is a growth mindset. Everyone is a mixture between fixed and growth mindsets. Some things are harder to be growth oriented about for many reasons. Receiving criticism, insecurity, defensiveness among other triggers, can cause a response that inhibits growth.

What surprised me in this exploration of growth mindset?

Dr Dweck’s work on growth mindset and in particular the key phrase of “not, yet” and how calling students “not, yet” instead of pass/fail like traditional education systems. And how this had a big impact on students learning and success (whatever that may be).

How will I intergrate growth mindset into my learning journey?

I really am trying to incorporate the “not yet” mindset into my everyday life. As a former ‘gifted student’ I have struggled a lot in my education journey with not being good enough, or smart enough as the other kids started to catch up to and pass my perceived intelligence. Knowing that brains are malleable and that you can literally learn and be good at anything (minus maybe some genetic factors in sports) is so helpful and mindset is so powerful.

Blending these concepts with my learning plan

I think it is important to have a growth mindset and understanding of neuroplasticity and what helps enable more nueroplasticity as it reinforces how important it is to take breaks from the work when blocked - go for a walk, go exercise, meditation etc. And helps when stuck and feeling frustrated to not take it out on myself as it’s all about learning and making and solidifying those pathways and not yet!
